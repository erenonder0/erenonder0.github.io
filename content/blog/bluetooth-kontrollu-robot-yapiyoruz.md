src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/418/1*aRZvTb1VlnMaZiU6vDAA2A.png" /></figure><h3>WHAT IS ARDUINO?</h3><p><strong>Arduino</strong> can be described as an open-source <strong>&#8220;BRAIN&#8221;</strong> built so that anyone interested in electronics can use it easily. <strong>With Arduino you can build </strong><em>blinking lights, systems driven by temperature sensors, line-following robots — in short, just about any electronics application you can think of. The only thing limiting what you can build with Arduino is </em><strong><em>your imagination</em></strong><em>. </em>You can build almost <strong>any</strong> electronic system you have in mind with <strong>Arduino</strong>.</p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1024/1*Vmt6yvTweW7ouXVBH64lZA.png" /><figcaption>A project built with Arduino</figcaption></figure><p><strong>Arduino</strong> is used to create standalone interactive objects, but it can also work alongside software running on a computer. In other words, you can connect it to many applications and develop freely. You can build with the <strong>Arduino</strong> IDE, which is platform independent — you can develop your projects on macOS, Linux or Windows.</p><p><strong>ARDUINO VARIANTS</strong></p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/710/1*eIfdF19Ck28XFQeh5d9qsA.png" /><figcaption>Arduino models</figcaption></figure><p>We now know what <strong>Arduino</strong> is. So which <strong>Arduino</strong> best suits our project? To answer that, you can browse the <strong>Arduino</strong> variants through the link below and find the board that fits your project best. In <strong>this project we will use the Arduino Uno</strong> board.</p><p><a href="http://www.dudigan.com/2015/12/arduino-modelleri-ve-ozellikleri/">Arduino Models and Their Specifications</a></p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/300/1*2y21zdVydYSs_CihSBpHIw.jpeg" /><figcaption><strong>L298N Motor Driver</strong></figcaption></figure><h3><strong>L298N Motor Driver Board:</strong></h3><p>The <strong>L298N</strong> motor driver board is designed to drive motors up to 24V. It has two channels and can supply 2A per channel. Because the board has two channels we can drive 2 motors — but by wiring the two motors on the right side of the car in <strong>reverse parallel</strong>, and the motors on the left side in <strong>reverse parallel</strong> as well, you can drive 4 motors.</p><h3>ASSEMBLING THE ROBOT PLATFORM</h3><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/696/1*Bz0ozhlN13FinfFcOrLUiw.jpeg" /><figcaption>PARTS OF THE ROBOT PLATFORM</figcaption></figure><p>Right, let's start assembling the robot parts. You can either follow the <strong>photographs below</strong> step by step, or follow along with the <strong>assembly video</strong> I will put at the end of this post.</p><p>The motors in the kit arrive with no wires attached, so let's start by soldering wires to them.</p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/696/1*XABJhKwPRkaje2wjwy-MSQ.jpeg" /><figcaption>Photograph taken from the Robotistan website.</figcaption></figure><p>When soldering, if you connect a <strong>red wire to the right terminal</strong> and a <strong>black wire to the left terminal</strong> of every motor, you will save yourself a lot of confusion later on.</p><p>Next, we mount the motors onto the tabs along the sides of the plate using the screws included in the kit, as shown below.</p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/696/1*qc_H2KM90emFr3o5YcZF5g.jpeg" /><figcaption>Photograph taken from the Robotistan website.</figcaption></figure><p>Once all the wheels are attached, we route the motor wires up through any of the holes in the plate. After that, all that remains is joining the plate holding the motors to the upper plate. You can do this easily using the yellow standoffs and screws that come with the kit. Once everything is done, the chassis is complete. If you did everything correctly, your body should look like the one below.</p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/696/1*eGCO-FBr6WtF3gfXXLn64Q.jpeg" /><figcaption>Photograph taken from the Robotistan website.</figcaption></figure><p>And that is the first step of our Bluetooth-controlled robot project. That is all for today. In Part Two I will be back with the remaining details, again <strong>with both photographs and video</strong>. Just be patient — I will publish Part Two as soon as I can. Take care :)</p>
